About the Journey

The Ranchi to Durgapur route covers 240 kilometers via NH33 and NH14, connecting Jharkhand with West Bengal's industrial hub. The journey takes approximately 5 hours with breaks. The route passes through scenic Purulia district and Bankura before reaching Durgapur, one of India's first planned industrial cities. The highway is well-maintained with good rest stops at Purulia and Bankura.

Durgapur is known for DSP (Durgapur Steel Plant) and various educational institutions. Our experienced drivers know this route well and can recommend good restaurants en route. All toll charges and parking fees are included in the fare. Popular route for both business travelers and students traveling to/from Durgapur.

Top Places to Visit in Durgapur

Make the most of your trip to Steel City of India

1

Durgapur Barrage

Beautiful dam on Damodar River built in 1955. Popular picnic spot with gardens and boat rides. Offers scenic views especially during sunset.

2

Troika Park

Large urban park with lake, walking tracks, and children's playground. Perfect for evening walks and family outings. Well-maintained with lush greenery.

3

Bhabani Pathak Teerth

Ancient Jain temple complex with beautiful architecture. Peaceful religious site with historical significance. Popular among pilgrims and architecture enthusiasts.

4

Garh Jungle

Dense forest area near Durgapur ideal for nature walks and bird watching. Home to various flora and fauna. Great escape for nature lovers.

5

Junction Mall

Modern shopping complex with multiplex, food court, and retail stores. Entertainment hub for shopping, dining, and cinema. Popular among youth and families.

6

Ram Mandir

Beautiful Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Rama. Well-maintained complex with gardens. Important religious landmark in Durgapur city center.
