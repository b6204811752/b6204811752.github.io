About the Journey

The Ranchi to Asansol route covers 220 kilometers via NH33 and Purulia Road, connecting Jharkhand with West Bengal's coal capital. The journey takes approximately 4.5 hours. Asansol is the second-largest city in West Bengal and an important industrial and educational hub. The route passes through scenic Purulia district with its red soil and unique landscape.

The highway is well-maintained with good food stops at Purulia and Raniganj. Our experienced drivers are familiar with this route and ensure a comfortable journey. All toll charges are included in the fare. Popular route for students traveling to medical colleges and engineering institutions in Asansol.

Top Places to Visit in Asansol

Make the most of your trip to West Bengal's industrial city

1

Kalyaneshwari Temple

Ancient Hindu temple dedicated to Goddess Kalyaneshwari, located 60 km from Asansol. Important religious site with beautiful surroundings. Popular pilgrimage destination.

2

Nehru Park

Large urban park in heart of Asansol with gardens, walking tracks, and children's play area. Perfect for evening strolls and family picnics.

3

Maithon Dam

Scenic dam on Barakar River, 50 km from Asansol. Boating facilities available. Popular weekend getaway with beautiful reservoir views and gardens.

4

Rahul Park

Peaceful park with lake and walking paths. Ideal for morning walks and yoga. Well-maintained with lush greenery and benches for relaxation.

5

City Centre Mall

Modern shopping complex with multiplex cinema, food court, and retail stores. Entertainment hub for shopping and dining. Popular hangout spot for youth.

6

Kazi Nazrul University

Renowned university named after Bengal's rebel poet. Beautiful campus with modern infrastructure. Attracts students from across Eastern India.
