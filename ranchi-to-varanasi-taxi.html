Places to Visit in Varanasi (Kashi)

Kashi Vishwanath Temple

Most sacred Shiva temple, one of 12 Jyotirlingas. Gold plated spires. Daily darshan 3 AM - 11 PM. Long queues during peak times - VIP darshan available for faster access. Nearby Kashi Vishwanath Corridor (opened 2021) - grand temple complex beautification. Divine atmosphere, chanting. Must-visit for every Hindu.

Ganga Aarti at Dashashwamedh Ghat

World-famous evening Ganga Aarti ceremony (6:30-7:15 PM). Spectacular ritual with lamps, bells, chanting. Thousands gather daily. Arrive 1 hour early for good spot. Early morning boat ride (5-7 AM) to witness sunrise, bathing rituals. Boat ride ₹200-500 per person. Deeply spiritual experience. Photography allowed.

84 Ghats

Chain of 84 ghats (riverfront steps) along Ganges. Assi Ghat (southern end), Dashashwamedh (central, most famous), Manikarnika Ghat (cremation - Hindu believe death in Kashi grants moksha). Morning boat tour covering multiple ghats recommended. Each ghat has unique history and rituals. Walking ghat tour possible.

Banaras Hindu University (BHU)

Asia's largest residential university (1916). Vishwanath Temple inside campus - beautiful architecture, less crowded alternative to main temple. Bharat Kala Bhawan Museum - ancient art collection. Sprawling green campus. Open to visitors. Good for peaceful visit away from crowded old city.

Sarnath (10 km)

Where Buddha gave first sermon after enlightenment. Major Buddhist pilgrimage site. Dhamek Stupa (128 ft) - 5th century. Ashoka Pillar ruins. Archaeological Museum - Lion Capital (India's national emblem). Mulagandha Kuti Vihar temple with beautiful frescoes. Peaceful, must-visit with Varanasi. Combine both in 2-day package.

Shopping & Banarasi Silk

Famous for Banarasi silk sarees - world renowned. Vishwanath Lane markets near temple. Thatheri Bazaar for brass items. Chowk area for street shopping. Genuine Banarasi silk expensive but authentic. Beware of fakes - visit government emporiums. Also famous for paan (betel leaf), lassi, street food - kachori, chaat, malaiyo (winter sweet).

It takes approximately 11-12 hours to reach Varanasi from Ranchi by taxi, covering a distance of 580 km via Gaya and Sasaram route. Comfortable highway journey with rest stops for meals and refreshments.

What is special about Varanasi?

Varanasi (Kashi/Banaras) is the holiest Hindu pilgrimage city and one of the world's oldest living cities (3000+ years). Famous for Kashi Vishwanath Jyotirlinga, spectacular Ganga Aarti, 84 ghats on sacred Ganges, spiritual atmosphere. Hindus believe death in Kashi grants moksha (liberation).

Do you provide Kashi darshan packages with Sarnath?

Yes, we offer 2-day/1-night, 3-day/2-night packages covering Kashi Vishwanath Temple, Ganga Aarti, boat ride, 84 ghats tour, Sarnath Buddhist site, and BHU campus. Can combine with Gaya and Bodh Gaya for complete Buddhist-Hindu pilgrimage circuit.

When is the best time to visit Varanasi?

Best time: October to March for pleasant weather (10-25°C). Avoid summer (April-June, 35-45°C) and monsoon (July-September, Ganges floods). Dev Deepawali (15 days after Diwali) most spectacular - all ghats lit with million lamps. Mahashivratri also very crowded - book 2-3 months advance.
